What is Port Entry and What to Expect??

Before landing the flight attendant will distribute customs declaration forms & immigration forms ( form I-94). Fill those out on the plane (Later after you land you have to submit those forms to the consent officer). Fill in the plane. After seeing your documents, immigration officer will indicate length of stay, university, etc. This will be attached to your passport. You must retain this I-94 form; else you will face trouble in leaving USA later. Once you have landed at Dallas Fort Worth airport go straight to the immigration counter. It might take 1hr there. Keep your I-20, passport, admission, aid letters, financial documents ready.

You should expect these question form them:

Is this your first time in the US?
Are you a student on F1 visa?
Which University are you joining?
After Verification they will I-94 card on one of the pages of your passport.

After That go collect your bags. If you are asked to open the baggage then do so slowly. In most of the case they will not ask so but if they do don't freak out and help officer to do their job by opening your baggage slowly.

Woooyaaa Your Port entry process is over
What should Keep in Your Cabin Bag?

Your Cabin Bag/Hand Baggage will the Most important baggage.
You Should Keep These Following items in your Cabin Bag:
  • All Old & new Passports
  • Admission Letter
  • I20
  • SEVIS Recepit
  • Transcripts
  • Test Scores(Sat, Toefl..)
  • Original Mark Sheet
  • Bank Statement
  • Affidavite of Support
  • Cash & Traveler's Cheque
  • Birth Certificate
  • Health Immuization Certificate
  • Driving License
  • Photographs
  • Laptop
  • Tablet
  • Phone
  • Pendrives
  • Headphones(Optional)
  • External HDD(Optional)
